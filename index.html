What Is Enclosed Auto Transport, Anyway?

I used to think car shipping was all the same—load it up and hit the road. But enclosed auto transport is a whole different level. Your car travels inside a fully closed trailer, protected from weather, debris, and even curious onlookers. It’s perfect for high-value vehicles like luxury cars, classics, or exotics. Unlike open transport, where cars are exposed, enclosed transport is like wrapping your car in a protective bubble.

Types of Enclosed Carriers I Discovered

Not all enclosed carriers are created equal, which was news to me. Here’s what I learned about the options:

  1. Hard-Side Carriers: These are fully enclosed trailers with solid walls, offering maximum protection. They often use hydraulic lifts, which was perfect for my low-clearance Corvette. They cost more, but the security is unmatched.
  2. Soft-Side Carriers: These have vinyl tarps on all sides, providing decent protection at a lower price. Your car might get a bit dusty, but it’s still safer than open transport.
  3. Liftgate Carriers: Designed for heavy or bulky items, these use liftgates for safe loading. They’re less common for cars but great for specific needs.

I went with a hard-side carrier for the ultimate protection, and it was worth every penny.

How Much Does It Cost?

One of my biggest questions was about cost. Enclosed transport is pricier than open transport, but I found it’s influenced by several factors:

  • Distance: Longer trips mean higher costs. For example, shipping from New York to Florida (about 1,200 miles) can cost around $1,307.
  • Vehicle Size: Bigger vehicles like SUVs cost more—sometimes $200–$500 extra.
  • Season: Summer and holidays can bump prices up by 10-20% due to demand.
  • Timing: Expedited shipping adds $500 or more. I saved by being flexible with my dates.
  • Enclosed vs. Open: Enclosed costs 40-60% more, but the protection justifies it.
